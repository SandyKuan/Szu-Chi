EchoPal: On Incorporating Humans in the Loop of Smart Speakers
Tools: Python, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Flask, MySQL, Amazon Web Service, Alexa Skill
Crowd-AI Lab, Penn State University Dr. Kenneth Huang 2019 ~ 2021

This project introduces EchoPal, a prototype system that allows a human receptionist to converse with the user synchronously via an Amazon’s Echo device without access to the user’s voice recordings. EchoPal (i) make sense of noisy automatic transcriptions (ii) quickly generate suggested responses to provide timely help and (iii) support quick search functions to handle complicated queries. We study end-to-end system latency and conversation quality with several pairs of participants, overall quality score is 3.71 / 5.0 from 17 participants and 88% of users considered it outperformed than or similarly good as Amazon’s Echo devices. Overall, our work demonstrates the challenges and potentials of human-in-the-loop voice-enabled devices, which opens up new possibilities for future smart devices and smart environments.
